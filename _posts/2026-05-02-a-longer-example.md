A slightly longer post to test how headers break up a page and to push the
reading-time estimate above one minute.

## Why minimal blogs work

The best technical blogs are mostly text. No cards, no thumbnails, no reading
progress bars. The reader came for the words; everything else is friction.

A few things that matter more than visual polish:

1. **Line length.** Around 650–700px keeps lines scannable.
2. **Line height.** Around 1.6 gives text room to breathe.
3. **Contrast.** Near-black on near-white, and the inverse in dark mode.

### Sub-sections

Sub-headers like this one are useful for long posts. Keep the hierarchy
shallow — if you need `h4`, the post probably wants to be two posts.

## Some filler to lengthen the estimate

Writing regularly matters more than writing perfectly. A post that ships at
80% is worth infinitely more than the perfect one still sitting in drafts.
Lower the bar for what counts as "worth publishing" — a short note about a bug
you fixed, a config that took an afternoon to figure out, a half-formed
opinion you want to revisit later. Future-you is the first audience; everyone
else is a bonus.

The mechanics should be boring: write Markdown, commit, push. If publishing
takes more than a minute of ceremony, the ceremony will win and the writing
will stop. That's the entire reason this site is a pile of static files.
